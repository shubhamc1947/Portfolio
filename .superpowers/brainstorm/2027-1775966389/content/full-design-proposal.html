01 / Site Architecture

Pages & Routes

Hybrid structure — cinematic landing scroll + dedicated subpages for depth.

Landing Page

/

The 60-second story. Hero → Philosophy → Featured Writing → Work Timeline → Skills → Contact. Each section is a scroll "chapter."

Writing

/writing

All articles — Medium imports + original pieces. Filterable by tag. Each article gets its own page with full content.

Case Study: Covrzy

/work/covrzy

Full narrative — the problem, your approach, architecture decisions, impact. Real engineering storytelling.

Case Study: Weknow

/work/weknow

Both roles combined into one story arc — intern to SDE. Government portals, ERPs, payment systems at scale.

Article Page

/writing/:slug

Individual article view — clean reading experience with typography optimized for long-form technical content.

About (optional)

/about

Extended bio, your philosophy, timeline. Could live on landing page only if you prefer fewer pages.

Technical decisions I'm making:

  • React Router added for multi-page navigation (currently missing — your site is a single scroll)
  • MDX or Markdown for blog articles — write once, render beautifully, no CMS overhead
  • Keep Vite + React — no need to switch to Next.js. Vite is fast, you know it, and SSG isn't critical for a portfolio
  • Framer Motion stays — but used intentionally for scroll-triggered reveals, not bouncy hover effects
  • SCSS → keep it, but restructure with design tokens (CSS variables for spacing, typography scale, colors)
